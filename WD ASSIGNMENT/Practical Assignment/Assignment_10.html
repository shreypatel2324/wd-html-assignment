Ahmedabad Plain Crash

An Air India Boeing 787 Dreamliner crashed shortly after takeoff from Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel International Airport in Ahmedabad on Thursday afternoon, killing 274 people: 241 passengers, 33 on-ground deaths. The aircraft, bound for London, plunged into the Meghani area of the city, sending up a massive plume of black smoke and triggering an immediate emergency response. According to the Directorate General of Civil Aviation (DGCA), there were 242 people on board the aircraft — 230 passengers, 2 pilots, and 10 cabin crew members.

Gujarat HM Harsh Sanhavi holds meeting with forensic lab officers on DNA effort

Gujarat Home Minister Harsh Sanghvi on Saturday held a high-level meeting with senior officials at the Forensic Science Laboratory (FSL) in Ahmedabad to review the ongoing investigation into the tragic Air India plane crash. Addressing to media after the meeting, Sanghvi said, “The Gujarat Forensic Science team has been working tirelessly for the past two days to match DNA samples collected from the crash victims and their family members.”
